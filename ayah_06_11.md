# Comparative Tafsir: Surah Al-Mulk, Ayahs 6-11

**Sources:** Ibn Kathir, al-Qurtubi, al-Zuhayli, al-Zamakhshari, al-Baghawi, Ibn Ashur, al-Maturidi, Safwat al-Tafasir

**Passage Theme:** The punishment of the disbelievers and the terrors of Hellfire, their dialogue with its keepers, and their belated confession.

---

## Ayah 6: وَلِلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا بِرَبِّهِمْ عَذَابُ جَهَنَّمَ وَبِئْسَ الْمَصِيرُ

### Core Meaning

This ayah extends the threat of punishment beyond the شياطين mentioned in the preceding verse (ayah 5) to all who disbelieve. Ibn Kathir explains the construction as: God "prepared" (أعتدنا) for those who disbelieve in their Lord the torment of جهنم, and what a wretched destination it is (بئس المآل والمنقلب). Al-Zamakhshari stresses the universalizing function: the punishment is not exclusive to the شياطين who are pelted with shooting stars, but extends to every كافر, whether from الإنس or الجن (Zamakhshari). Al-Zuhayli likewise notes that the ayah generalizes the وعيد: the عذاب is for every جاحد بربه, every مكذّب لرسله, from both الجن and الإنس (Zuhayli). Ibn Ashur identifies this as a تتميم, a rhetorical completion to prevent the listener from assuming the عذاب is for الشياطين alone (Ibn Ashur). Al-Maturidi reads المصير as الطريق, i.e. "wretched is the path" of one who walks it toward the السعير (Maturidi).

### Linguistic Analysis

- **مفردات:** المصير from صار / يصير, meaning the place or state one ends up in. Most mufassirun gloss it as المآل والمنقلب (the final destination and return). Al-Maturidi uniquely glosses it as الطريق (the path), suggesting the journey itself, not just its terminus.
- **إعراب:** Ibn Ashur notes that تقديم المجرور (وللذين) serves الاهتمام بتعلقه بالمسند إليه والمبادرة به, i.e. fronting for emphasis and urgency. The sentence وبئس المصير is either حال or جملة معترضة for إنشاء الذم. The مخصوص بالذم (the thing condemned) is elided, understood as عذاب جهنم (Ibn Ashur).
- **بلاغة:** Safwat al-Tafasir identifies a مقابلة (antithetical parallelism) between this ayah and the later verse (12): وللذين كفروا بربهم عذاب جهنم is contrasted with إن الذين يخشون ربهم بالغيب لهم مغفرة وأجر كبير (Safwat). Al-Zuhayli similarly notes this as a مقابلة (Zuhayli).

### Points of Divergence

**Issue: Relationship to the preceding ayah (ayah 5)**
- View 1 (Zamakhshari, Zuhayli, Safwat): The waw is عاطفة, connecting this sentence to وأعتدنا لهم عذاب السعير. The shift from السعير to جهنم signals a broadening of scope from الشياطين to all الكفار.
- View 2 (Ibn Ashur): The sentence is a تتميم (rhetorical completion), and the waw is conjunctive. The change in wording (from عذاب السعير to عذاب جهنم) is deliberate to signal a new, wider audience of the threat.
- View 3 (Zamakhshari, variant reading): A قراءة reading عذاب جهنم بالنصب exists, making it an عطف on عذاب السعير in ayah 5.

**Issue: Who are الذين كفروا بربهم?**
- View 1 (Zamakhshari): All who disbelieve, including شياطين الإنس والجن.
- View 2 (Zuhayli): Specifically الجاحدين بربهم المكذبين رسله من الجن والإنس, the deniers and those who call the messengers liars.
- View 3 (Ibn Ashur): عامة المشركين, the general body of polytheists.

### Unique Insights

- Al-Maturidi's reading of المصير as الطريق (the path) rather than المآل (the destination) is distinctive. It reframes the condemnation: the wretchedness is in the entire trajectory of disbelief, not only its endpoint.
- Ibn Ashur's precise grammatical observation that the مخصوص بالذم after بئس is elided (حذف المخصوص بالذم لدلالة ما قبل بئس عليه) is a point of إعراب refinement not found in the other tafasir.

## Ayah 7: إِذَا أُلْقُوا فِيهَا سَمِعُوا لَهَا شَهِيقًا وَهِيَ تَفُورُ

### Core Meaning

When the disbelievers are hurled into جهنم, as fuel is thrown into a great fire, they hear from it a شهيق, a horrifying, grating sound, while it boils and seethes. Ibn Kathir, citing Ibn Jarir, defines the شهيق as الصياح (a scream or wail). Al-Thawri explains تفور as: "it boils with them as grain boils in abundant water" (Ibn Kathir). Al-Qurtubi transmits from Ibn Abbas a vivid image: جهنم shrieks toward the كفار the way a mule brays at barley (تشهق إليهم شهقة البغلة للشعير), then it exhales a زفرة so terrifying that no one remains unafraid (Qurtubi). The verb ألقوا (passive: "they are cast") conveys the utter powerlessness of the كفار, thrown like حطب (firewood) into the blaze (Zamakhshari, Safwat).

### Linguistic Analysis

- **مفردات:**
  - شهيق: technically the intake of breath in the chest (تردد الأنفاس في الصدر), as opposed to زفير which occurs in the throat (Qurtubi). Ibn Ashur explains it is applied to the sound of جهنم's flames by way of تفظيع (making it horrifying), because the لام in سمعوا لها indicates that the شهيق belongs to the fire itself (شبه ملك) (Ibn Ashur).
  - تفور: from فار / يفور, meaning to boil, to seethe. The image is of a مرجل (cauldron) boiling violently (all sources).
- **إعراب:** Ibn Ashur notes that إذا is a ظرف متعلق بـ سمعوا, indicating the simultaneity (اقتران) between the moment of إلقاء and the hearing of the شهيق. The sentence وهي تفور is a حال من ضمير فيها (Ibn Ashur).
- **بلاغة:** Al-Zuhayli and Safwat identify a استعارة مكنية (implied metaphor): the fire's intense sound is likened to the شهيق of a living creature in distress, specifically the braying of a donkey, the most repulsive of sounds. The شبه المكني is the animate creature, and the لازم retained is the شهيق (Safwat, Zuhayli).

### Points of Divergence

**Issue: Whose شهيق is it?**
- View 1 (Ibn Abbas, majority): The شهيق belongs to جهنم itself, not to its inhabitants. The fire screams as they enter it (Qurtubi, Zamakhshari possibility 3).
- View 2 (Ata'): The شهيق is from the كفار themselves upon being thrown in (Qurtubi).
- View 3 (Zamakhshari): The شهيق could be from the earlier inhabitants already in the fire (ممن تقدم طرحهم فيها), or from the newcomers themselves.
- View 4 (Maturidi): Both readings are plausible. The repugnant sound is an observable phenomenon regardless of whether the source has consciousness or not. The صوت المنكر is equally terrifying from an inanimate source as from an animate one (Maturidi).

### Unique Insights

- Al-Maturidi offers a subtle epistemological point: we need not determine the source of the شهيق precisely, because a horrifying sound terrifies whether it comes from a being that "knows" sound or from one that does not. This is a characteristic Maturidi move, avoiding unnecessary speculation on غيب details.
- Al-Qurtubi's citation of Ibn Abbas comparing the شهيق to the شهقة البغلة للشعير (the bray of a mule at barley) is uniquely vivid, found only in his tafsir. It suggests the fire has an appetite, a craving for its fuel.

## Ayah 8: تَكَادُ تَمَيَّزُ مِنَ الْغَيْظِ كُلَّمَا أُلْقِيَ فِيهَا فَوْجٌ سَأَلَهُمْ خَزَنَتُهَا أَلَمْ يَأْتِكُمْ نَذِيرٌ

### Core Meaning

The fire nearly splits apart from sheer rage. Every time a group of disbelievers is cast into it, the keepers of Hell ask them, with مocking rebuke: did no warner come to you? Ibn Kathir explains تميّز as: "its parts nearly separate from one another" (ينفصل بعضها من بعض) due to the intensity of its غيظ and حنق against the كفار (Ibn Kathir). Al-Zamakhshari connects this to Arabic idiom: "so-and-so nearly splits with غيظ" (يتميز غيظا ويتقصف غضبا), and "he raged until a piece of him flew into the earth and a piece into the sky" (Zamakhshari). Ibn Ashur identifies this as a تمثيلية مكنية (a compound implied metaphor): the fire's state of seething, leaping, and consuming is likened to a مغتاظ شديد الغيظ, a furious being that leaves nothing within its reach unharmed (Ibn Ashur). The question أَلَمْ يَأْتِكُمْ نَذِيرٌ is not for information but for توبيخ وتقريع (rebuke and reproach), adding حسرة upon حسرة to their torment (all sources).

### Linguistic Analysis

- **مفردات:**
  - تميّز: أصله تتميّز, with the deletion of one تاء for lightness. It means تتقطع / تنفصل / تنشق (to split, to fragment). Ibn Qutayba glosses it as تنشق (to crack open) (Baghawi).
  - الغيظ: أشد الغضب, the most extreme form of anger (Ibn Ashur). Al-Zuhayli defines it as شدة الغضب (Zuhayli).
  - فوج: جماعة, a group or throng (all sources). Ibn Ashur notes the ضمائر العائدة refer to the فوج with جمع (سألهم) because the فوج is interpreted as a جماعة أفراده (the individuals composing the group) (Ibn Ashur).
  - خزنة: plural of خازن, those entrusted with guarding. Here it means مالك وأعوانه من الزبانية, the angel Malik and his assistants (Zamakhshari, Zuhayli). Ibn Ashur explains the term is مجاز مرسل: خازن originally means one who stores something in a safe place, applied to the guardians of Hell by extension (Ibn Ashur).
  - نذير: المنذر, the warner. It functions here as وصف for a رسول (Zamakhshari, Ibn Ashur). Al-Zamakhshari notes it can also be a مصدر (infinitive) meaning الإنذار (the act of warning), on the pattern of the رسول being so identified with his mission that he "is" the warning itself (Zamakhshari).
- **إعراب:** Ibn Ashur explains كلما as a compound of كل (totality) and ما (المصدرية الظرفية). The construction acquires the meaning of شرط (conditionality), needing two sentences, one contingent on the other. Both ألقي and سألهم are in the ماضي because after كلما, the past tense is preferred for its lightness, while the future meaning is understood from context (Ibn Ashur).
- **بلاغة:** Safwat and al-Zuhayli both identify the استعارة المكنية in تكاد تميز من الغيظ: جهنم is likened to an enraged person about to burst, with the مشبه به (the furious person) suppressed and only its لازم (الغيظ, the attribute of rage) retained (Safwat, Zuhayli). The استفهام in ألم يأتكم is إنكاري للتوبيخ والتبكيت (Zuhayli, Safwat, Ibn Ashur).

### Points of Divergence

**Issue: Is the غيظ literal or figurative?**
- View 1 (Majority: Zamakhshari, Ibn Ashur, Zuhayli, Safwat): It is تمثيل / استعارة مكنية, a figurative depiction. The fire does not literally feel anger. Its violent seething is represented through the idiom of a furiously angry person.
- View 2 (Maturidi): God can endow جهنم (and any inanimate thing) with genuine غضب and awareness of His majesty. It is within God's power to make the fire truly rage against His enemies, just as He made it welcoming to Ibrahim. This is not mere metaphor; it may be a real attribute bestowed on it. Al-Maturidi draws a moral lesson: if even جهنم rages against the enemies of God, then the believers (أولياء) have even greater obligation to feel شدة on behalf of God against His enemies (Maturidi).
- View 3 (Zamakhshari, secondary): The غيظ might belong to الزبانية (the angelic guards), not to the fire itself (Zamakhshari).

**Issue: Who are the خزنة?**
- All agree they are مالك and his assistants (الزبانية). No significant divergence.

### Unique Insights

- Al-Maturidi's moral-theological reflection is remarkable: he draws an analogy from the fire's غيظ to the duty of believers. If an inanimate creation rages on behalf of God, then the أولياء have even more reason to show شدة على الكفار, citing the Quranic praise أشداء على الكفار رحماء بينهم (Maturidi). This ethical derivation is unique to his tafsir.
- Al-Maturidi also notes a second حكمة: God describes the شدة of the fire so that people cannot claim on the Day of Judgment, إنا كنا عن هذا غافلين ("we were heedless of this") (Maturidi).
- Ibn Ashur's comparison of this استعارة to the Quranic جداراً يريد أن ينقض (a wall that "wants" to collapse, al-Kahf 77) is an elegant parallel, showing how the Quran personifies inanimate objects through the attribution of volition or emotion (Ibn Ashur).

## Ayah 9: قَالُوا بَلَى قَدْ جَاءَنَا نَذِيرٌ فَكَذَّبْنَا وَقُلْنَا مَا نَزَّلَ اللَّهُ مِنْ شَيْءٍ إِنْ أَنْتُمْ إِلَّا فِي ضَلَالٍ كَبِيرٍ

### Core Meaning

The disbelievers answer: Yes, a warner did come to us, but we denied him and told him God has not revealed anything; you (the messengers) are in nothing but great error. Ibn Kathir frames this as evidence of God's عدل (justice): He never punishes without first establishing the حجة (proof) through the sending of رسل, as confirmed by وما كنا معذبين حتى نبعث رسولا (al-Isra 15) (Ibn Kathir). Al-Zamakhshari highlights that the بلى is an اعتراف بعدل الله (acknowledgment of God's justice), and an إقرار that God removed every excuse (أزاح عللهم) through the بعثة الرسل (Zamakhshari). Al-Maturidi adds a psychological dimension: in the آخرة, the كفار first tried lying under oath (والله ربنا ما كنا مشركين), hoping it might help them as it had in the دنيا. When that failed and they were cast into the fire, they resorted to honesty and صدق, hoping الاعتراف might spare them (Maturidi).

### Linguistic Analysis

- **مفردات:**
  - بلى: the particle that negates the negation in the preceding استفهام (ألم يأتكم). It affirms: "Yes, indeed a warner came." Ibn Ashur notes that the subsequent قد جاءنا نذير is تأكيد (emphatic confirmation) of what بلى already conveys, a form of تكرير الكلام عند التحسر (repetition born of remorse) (Ibn Ashur).
  - ما نزّل الله من شيء: the من is زائدة for تأكيد النفي (intensifying the negation). The عموم of شيء indicates they denied the very possibility of divine revelation to any human, echoing ما أنزل الله على بشر من شيء (al-An'am 91) (Ibn Ashur).
  - ضلال كبير: Ibn Ashur explains كبير as شديد بالغ غاية ما يبلغ إليه جنسه, as if the ضلال were a physical body of great size (Ibn Ashur). The ظرفية (في ضلال) is مجازية, likening their immersion in error to being enclosed in a container (Ibn Ashur).

### Points of Divergence

**Issue: Who says إن أنتم إلا في ضلال كبير?**
- View 1 (Majority: Zamakhshari primary, Zuhayli, Safwat, Qurtubi, Baghawi): It is part of the كفار's speech to the رسل, recounting what they used to say in the دنيا. They told the messengers: "You are in nothing but great error."
- View 2 (Zamakhshari alternative, Maturidi possibility, Qurtubi variant): It is the speech of الخزنة to the كفار, spoken at the moment of their entry into the fire, meaning: "You were in nothing but great error."
- View 3 (Zamakhshari, further alternative): It is the speech of الرسل to the كفار, which the كفار are now recounting to the خزنة: "The messengers told us this, but we did not accept it."
- View 4 (Ibn Ashur, preferred): It is the خزنة's continuation of their speech. The interposition of the فوج's answer (بلى قد جاءنا نذير) was an اعتراض (parenthetical interruption) driven by the شدة الخوف of the فوج, who rushed to confess. The خزنة then conclude with إن أنتم إلا في ضلال كبير. This is supported by the re-introduction of the verb قالوا in ayah 10 (وقالوا لو كنا), which would be unnecessary if the كفار were still speaking continuously (Ibn Ashur).

**Issue: The meaning of القصر in إن أنتم إلا في ضلال كبير**
- Ibn Ashur identifies this as a قصر قلب (a restrictive reversal): "Your condition is nothing other than ضلال, and it is not الوحي الإلهي and الهدى as you claim" (Ibn Ashur).

### Unique Insights

- Al-Maturidi's psychological reading is striking: the كفار's trajectory in the آخرة begins with lies (ما كنا مشركين), proceeds to honest admission when they realize lying is futile, but even their honesty cannot help them. This maps a progression of desperation: denial, then confession, then despair (Maturidi).
- Ibn Ashur's argument that إن أنتم إلا في ضلال كبير belongs to the خزنة (not to the كفار's recollection of their words to the رسل) is a minority but well-argued position, supported by the grammatical evidence of the re-introduced وقالوا in ayah 10.

## Ayah 10: وَقَالُوا لَوْ كُنَّا نَسْمَعُ أَوْ نَعْقِلُ مَا كُنَّا فِي أَصْحَابِ السَّعِيرِ

### Core Meaning

The disbelievers then say among themselves, in remorse: had we truly listened or used our reason, we would not be among the people of the السعير. Ibn Kathir explains: had they had عقول that benefited them, or had they listened to what God revealed of الحق, they would not have persisted in كفر بالله. But they had neither فهم to grasp what the رسل brought, nor عقل to guide them to follow (Ibn Kathir). Al-Zuhayli notes that السمع is mentioned before العقل because the المدعو first hears the داعية's words, then reflects upon them (Zuhayli). Ibn Ashur elaborates: السمع here is not mere physical hearing (which they obviously possessed) but سمع خاص, the hearing of one who genuinely receives and considers, and العقل is عقل خاص, the reasoning of one who actually deliberates. Their إعراض (turning away) from the رسل's call functioned as if they did not hear at all, and their refusal to تدبّر functioned as if they had no عقل (Ibn Ashur).

### Linguistic Analysis

- **مفردات:**
  - نسمع: سماع تفهم (Zuhayli), سماع من يعي ويتفكر (Zamakhshari, Zajjaj via Baghawi), hearing with comprehension and openness.
  - نعقل: عقل تفكر (Zuhayli), عقل من يميز وينظر (Qurtubi, Baghawi), reasoning that leads to discernment and action.
  - أصحاب السعير: those who belong to the blazing fire. في here means في عدادهم ومن جملتهم, "in their company and among their number" (Zuhayli).
- **إعراب:** أو is للتقسيم (for division), according to Ibn Ashur, distributing between the two faculties that could have saved them: حسن الاستماع (good reception) and حسن التفهم والنظر (good reflection) (Ibn Ashur).
- **بلاغة:** Safwat identifies the repetition of أصحاب السعير across ayah 10 and ayah 11 as إطناب بتكرار الجملة for emphasis and تنبيه (Safwat).

### Points of Divergence

**Issue: Does this negate that they had سمع and عقل at all?**
- View 1 (Maturidi, preferred): It does not. They had already admitted بلى قد جاءنا نذير, confirming they heard and understood. The negation is of الانتفاع (benefit), not of the faculties themselves. They heard but did not respond (لم يجيبوا لما سمعوا), and they understood but did not act (لم يقوموا بوفاء ما عقلوا) (Maturidi).
- View 2 (Some scholars, cited by Maturidi): It means "had we heard in the دنيا the way we now hear (in the آخرة), or reasoned the way we now reason." Al-Maturidi rejects this, arguing that the آخرة is not a دار إسماع وإفهام (a realm of instruction and comprehension), so this interpretation is not sound (Maturidi).
- View 3 (Qurtubi): The ayah proves that the كافر was given no genuine عقل at all (لم يُعطَ من العقل شيئا). This is the stronger claim, which al-Qurtubi supports by cross-reference to his discussion in Surat al-Tur (Qurtubi).

**Issue: Is the sequence نسمع / نعقل significant?**
- View 1 (Zuhayli): السمع precedes because the مدعو first hears the caller, then reflects.
- View 2 (Ibn Ashur): السمع is to العقل as the جزئي is to the كلي. It is also the natural temporal order: one first hears the نذير's call, then applies عقل to it.
- View 3 (Zamakhshari): The مدار التكليف (basis of religious obligation) rests on two pillars: أدلة السمع (scriptural evidence) and أدلة العقل (rational evidence) (Zamakhshari).

### Unique Insights

- Al-Zamakhshari dismisses as بدع التفاسير (heretical interpretations) the claim that نسمع refers to أصحاب الحديث (the Hadith school) and نعقل to أصحاب الرأي (the rationalist school). He mocks this anachronism sharply: "as if this ayah was revealed after these two schools appeared" (Zamakhshari). Ibn Ashur notes this and adds that he could not identify who originally proposed this interpretation (Ibn Ashur).
- Ibn Ashur transmits a حديث (via al-Qurtubi from al-Hakim al-Tirmidhi) in which a man praised a Christian's intelligence before the Prophet, who rebuked him: مه، إن الكافر لا عقل له, citing this very ayah. Ibn Ashur, however, notes he could not verify this hadith and that al-Suyuti did not include it in his مأثور for either Surat al-Tur or Surat al-Mulk (Ibn Ashur).
- Al-Zuhayli draws a فقهي point from this ayah: الدين لا يتم إلا بالتعليم (religion is not complete without instruction), because the السمع implies the guidance of a مرشد. He also notes an argument for تفضيل السمع على البصر (the superiority of hearing over sight), since the ayah makes السمع (not البصر) the مناط الفوز (pivot of salvation) (Zuhayli).

## Ayah 11: فَاعْتَرَفُوا بِذَنْبِهِمْ فَسُحْقًا لِأَصْحَابِ السَّعِيرِ

### Core Meaning

They confessed their sin, and so: "Away with the people of the السعير!" The اعتراف comes when it no longer benefits them: they admit their كفر and تكذيب of the رسل, but this admission cannot save them. Ibn Kathir concludes: "They blamed themselves and felt remorse where remorse could not help them" (عادوا على أنفسهم بالملامة وندموا حيث لا تنفعهم الندامة). He cites two prophetic traditions: "People will not perish until they have been given full excuse against themselves" (لن يهلك الناس حتى يعذروا من أنفسهم, Ahmad), and "No one enters the Fire except that he knows the Fire is more fitting for him than الجنة" (لا يدخل أحد النار إلا وهو يعلم أن النار أولى به من الجنة, Ahmad/Sahih) (Ibn Kathir). Al-Zuhayli frames this as بيان بالجريمة ثم العقاب: the crime is stated, then the sentence follows (Zuhayli).

### Linguistic Analysis

- **مفردات:**
  - اعترفوا: from اعتراف, إقرار عن معرفة, an admission that proceeds from knowledge, not mere verbal concession (Zuhayli).
  - ذنبهم: singular, but meaning الجمع (the plural, i.e. "their sins"). Al-Zuhayli explains two reasons for the singular form: (a) when a مصدر is added to a plural (إضافة المصدر إلى الجمع), the addition to the collective suffices for the plural meaning; (b) ذنب is a مصدر, and المصدر يصلح للواحد والجمع (Zuhayli). The specific ذنب is الكفر وتكذيب الأنبياء (Zuhayli, Qurtubi).
  - سحقا: an اسم مصدر meaning البعد (remoteness, distance). The root س ح ق carries the sense of extreme remoteness. Al-Zuhayli explains: أسحقهم الله سحقا, "God distanced them with an extreme distancing" (Zuhayli). Ibn Ashur refines: it is a مفعول مطلق نائب عن فعله, i.e. the verb أسحقهم الله is understood, and سحقا takes its place as a دعاء (imprecation) (Ibn Ashur).
- **إعراب:** Al-Zuhayli notes سحقا is منصوب على المصدر, either as a substitute for the verb (بدل من الفعل) or governed by an implied verb: ألزمهم الله سحقا (Zuhayli). Ibn Ashur identifies two فاء's: الفاء الأولى فصيحة (an "eloquent fa'" implying an elided protasis: "since they said all that, it has become clear that they confessed"), and الفاء الثانية للتسبب (causal: "therefore, away with them") (Ibn Ashur).
- **قراءات:** The majority reads فسُحْقاً with سكون الحاء. Al-Kisa'i and Abu Ja'far read it فسُحُقاً with ضم الحاء, which is a variant لغة, similar to الرعُب / الرعْب and السحُت / السحْت (Baghawi, Qurtubi, Ibn Ashur).

### Points of Divergence

**Issue: Is سحقا a دعاء (curse) or a تعجب (expression of wonder)?**
- View 1 (Majority: Ibn Kathir, Qurtubi, Zuhayli, Baghawi, Safwat): It is a دعاء عليهم, an imprecation: "May God distance them from His mercy."
- View 2 (Ibn Ashur, alternative): It may be used for تعجيب, expressing wonder at their plight, in the way one says قاتله الله or ويل له in contexts of astonishment rather than literal cursing (Ibn Ashur).
- View 3 (Ibn Ashur, further possibility): It may be something said to them يوم الحساب (on the Day of Reckoning) after their اعتراف, as a تنديم (deepening of remorse) that adds psychological ألم beyond the physical حريق (Ibn Ashur).

**Issue: Is السحق a place?**
- View 1 (Majority): سحق is an abstract noun meaning بعد (distance/remoteness from God's mercy).
- View 2 (Sa'id ibn Jubayr, Abu Salih, via Qurtubi): السحق is a specific واد في جهنم (a valley in Hell) called al-Suhq (Qurtubi).
- View 3 (Maturidi): سحقا is purely a دعاء meaning بعدا, on the pattern of an imprecation against them (Maturidi).

**Issue: Who is the speaker of فسحقا?**
- All agree this is God's pronouncement, not the speech of the كفار or the خزنة.

### Unique Insights

- Ibn Ashur's layered analysis of the two فاء's (الفصيحة and التسببية) is the most detailed grammatical treatment of this ayah across all eight sources.
- The variant interpretation of السحق as a واد في جهنم (Qurtubi, from Sa'id ibn Jubayr and Abu Salih) transforms what the majority reads as an abstract دعاء into a concrete geographical detail of Hell's topography.
- Ibn Ashur's observation that لأصحاب السعير functions as a تذييل (a generalizing conclusion) is significant: the curse applies not just to those being addressed, but to all أصحاب السعير across all nations, giving the statement a universal scope (Ibn Ashur).
- Al-Zamakhshari emphasizes that the اعتراف or جحود makes no difference: سواء اعترفوا أو جحدوا, فإن ذلك لا ينفعهم. The sentence stands regardless of their confession (Zamakhshari).

## Cross-Ayah Observations

### Thematic Arc (Ayahs 6-11)
The passage follows a carefully structured dramatic sequence:
1. **Declaration of punishment** (6): the general عذاب for all كفار.
2. **Sensory horror** (7): the sound and fury of جهنم as the كفار enter.
3. **Personification and dialogue** (8): the fire's rage, and the خزنة's rebuke.
4. **Confession of guilt** (9): the كفار admit they were warned.
5. **Self-blame** (10): they lament their own failure to listen and think.
6. **Divine verdict** (11): the final سحقا, sealing their fate.

### Key Theological Principle
All eight mufassirun converge on the principle of عدل الله: God does not punish without prior إنذار. The dialogue between the خزنة and the كفار is proof that الحجة was established, الرسل were sent, and the كفار rejected them by choice. This principle is anchored in وما كنا معذبين حتى نبعث رسولا (al-Isra 15), cited by Ibn Kathir, al-Zuhayli, and others.

### Distinctive Contributions by Source
- **Ibn Kathir:** Hadith-rich, focused on prophetic traditions that reinforce the theological message. His treatment is concise and narration-driven.
- **Al-Qurtubi:** Rich in فقهي derivations and لغوي detail, with extensive citation of early authorities (Ibn Abbas, Mujahid, Sa'id ibn Jubayr). Uniquely transmits the شهقة البغلة للشعير image.
- **Al-Zamakhshari:** The most linguistically penetrating. His بلاغة analysis (استعارة مكنية, تمثيل) and his attention to the multiple possible speakers of إن أنتم إلا في ضلال كبير make his tafsir indispensable for rhetorical study. His المعتزلي theological commitments surface in his emphasis on the كفار's free choice and his rejection of الجبر.
- **Al-Zuhayli:** Systematic and structured. His four-fold classification of the fire's attributes (شهيق, فوران, غيظ, سؤال الخزنة) and his فقه الحياة sections provide clear pedagogical organization.
- **Al-Baghawi:** Conservative and narrator-based, closely following earlier authorities. His treatment is the most concise but reliably transmits the core tradition.
- **Ibn Ashur:** The most grammatically rigorous. His إعراب analysis (تقديم المجرور, الفاء الفصيحة, كلما as شرط) and his بلاغة taxonomy (تمثيلية مكنية, قصر قلب) are the most technically refined across all sources.
- **Al-Maturidi:** Theologically distinctive. His reflections on whether the fire's غيظ is literal or metaphorical, his moral derivation (the duty of believers to feel شدة for God's sake), and his psychological analysis of the كفار's progression from lying to confession are unique contributions.
- **Safwat al-Tafasir:** A synthesis that excels in identifying البديع and البلاغة devices (استعارة مكنية, مقابلة, إطناب, سجع مرصع). It is the most accessible for students of Arabic rhetoric.
