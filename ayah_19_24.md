# Comparative Tafsir: Surah Al-Mulk, Ayahs 19-24

**Sources consulted:** Ibn Kathir, al-Qurtubi, al-Zuhayli, al-Zamakhshari, al-Baghawi, Ibn Ashur, al-Maturidi, Safwat al-Tafasir

---

## Ayah 19: أَوَلَمْ يَرَوْا إِلَى الطَّيْرِ فَوْقَهُمْ صَافَّاتٍ وَيَقْبِضْنَ مَا يُمْسِكُهُنَّ إِلَّا الرَّحْمَنُ إِنَّهُ بِكُلِّ شَيْءٍ بَصِيرٌ

### Core Meaning

This ayah directs attention to the phenomenon of birds in flight as a proof of God's exclusive power and sovereignty over creation. The Quran asks: have they not looked at the birds above them, spreading their wings and folding them? None holds them aloft except the Most Merciful. The passage transitions from proofs drawn from human experience on earth (the preceding ayah on the ذلول earth) to the more wondrous spectacle of creatures sustained in a medium that, by nature, does not support weight (Ibn Ashur). As al-Maturidi observes, the air (الهواء) is not a resting-place that holds objects as the earth and sky do; that birds remain aloft despite their heaviness is therefore evidence of God's لطف and قدرة. Ibn Kathir explains that God holds them in the air بما سخر لهن من الهواء, by the subjugation of the air itself, out of His رحمة and لطف. Al-Zamakhshari details the mechanics: when birds spread their wings they align the flight feathers (القوادم) in a row, and when they draw them in, they strike their flanks (جنوبهن), and God sustains them through both motions by virtue of His power and the physical design He gave them: the القوادم (ten primary feathers per wing), the خوافي (secondary feathers), and the aerodynamic shape of their bodies. Ibn Ashur adds that God's إمساك consists in the خصائص He embedded in their creation: lightness of bones, strength of the جوانح (thoracic muscles), the القوادم and خوافي, and the body shape that enables passage through air. He pointedly notes that birds are not suspended by invisible strings like a conjurer's props, but fly by intrinsic God-given design. Safwat al-Tafasir synthesizes: just as God made the earth ذلول for humans, He made the air ذلول for birds, and none holds them from falling except God (al-Razi, cited by Safwat).

### Linguistic Analysis

**Key vocabulary:**

- **صافّات** (root: ص ف ف): literally "ones that line up in a row." Applied to birds, it means باسطات أجنحتهن, spreading their wings so the feathers form an aligned row (al-Zamakhshari, Ibn Ashur). Al-Zamakhshari explains that when a bird extends (بسط) its wing, the قوادم are صفّ (lined up evenly), hence the bird is called صافّة.
- **يقبضن** (root: ق ب ض): the opposite of بسط. Here: folding the wings back, drawing them toward the body (al-Qurtubi: يضربن بها جنوبهن). Al-Baghawi: قبض الأجنحة بعد البسط.
- **يمسكهنّ** (root: م س ك): holds them, prevents them from falling. Al-Maturidi reports and refutes Ja'far ibn Harb's claim that إمساك here is a metaphor for تعليم (teaching). Al-Maturidi argues decisively that the word إمساك demands an actual act of holding at the moment described, not a prior act of instruction. One cannot say "I sewed that garment" merely because one taught someone else to sew.
- **الرحمن**: Ibn Ashur notes the deliberate choice of الرحمن over the لفظ الجلالة (Allah). In the parallel verse in Surat al-Nahl (16:79), the name الله is used. Here, الرحمن appears because the Quraysh specifically rejected this name, and the surah uses it four times as a pointed reminder.
- **بصير** (root: ب ص ر): Ibn Ashur distinguishes this from the الاسم الحسنى "السميع البصير." Here بصير is derived from بصيرة (insight), meaning عليم (all-knowing), i.e., He knows how to create and how to engineer wonders. It is an خبر (predicate), not a وصف (divine attribute in the technical theological sense).

**Grammatical points (i'rab):**

- **فوقهم**: منصوب على الحال from الطير (Ibn Ashur, al-Zuhayli).
- **صافّات**: حال منصوب, since the رؤية here is رؤية العين (ocular), not رؤية القلب (al-Zuhayli).
- **ويقبضن**: عطف on صافّات. The جملة is in موضع الحال, with a تقدير of قابضات (al-Zuhayli). This is عطف الفعل المضارع على اسم الفاعل, permitted because of the مشابهة between them (al-Zuhayli, Ibn Ashur).

**Rhetorical devices (balagha):**

- **طباق** between صافّات and يقبضن (بسط vs. قبض), noted by al-Zuhayli and Safwat.
- **العدول من الاسم إلى الفعل**: The shift from the nominal صافّات to the verbal يقبضن is the central rhetorical feature. Al-Zamakhshari provides the definitive explanation: بسط الجناح is the أصل (primary state) in flight, comparable to مدّ الأطراف in swimming. Since it is the stable, continuous condition, it receives the اسم الفاعل form (indicating ثبات). القبض is طارئ (secondary, intermittent), done for الاستظهار على التحرك (gaining momentum), so it receives the فعل المضارع form (indicating تجدّد and حدوث). Ibn Ashur elaborates: the مضارع is chosen to استحضار (make vivid) the wondrous moment when the bird reverses its wing-beat and thereby gains renewed thrust against gravity.
- **الاستفهام الإنكاري**: أولم يروا is إنكاري (Ibn Ashur, al-Zuhayli), treating the addressees as if they had not observed these phenomena, because they failed to derive the proper lesson from them.
- **الإطناب**: Ibn Ashur notes that this ayah is more expansive than its نظير in Surat al-Nahl (16:79), adding three descriptive elements (فوقهم, صافّات, يقبضن) absent from the earlier verse. This إطناب is deliberate: the Quraysh did not heed the concise version, so the fuller description was given to awaken their attention.

### Points of Divergence

**Issue: The meaning of إمساك (what "holding" means)**
- View 1 (Majority, including al-Zamakhshari, Ibn Kathir, al-Baghawi, al-Zuhayli, Safwat, Ibn Ashur): God literally sustains the birds in the air through His قدرة, by designing their bodies, feathers, and the aerodynamics that prevent them from falling. The إمساك is a real, ongoing divine act.
- View 2 (Ja'far ibn Harb, reported and refuted by al-Maturidi): الإمساك is a كناية عن التعليم (metonymy for teaching), meaning God taught birds how to fly, but the act of staying aloft is their own. Al-Maturidi rejects this at length: if إمساك meant only "prior instruction," then any craftsman could claim credit for his student's work, which is linguistically and logically untenable. The community (الأمة) understood ما يمسكهن إلا الرحمن as equivalent to ما خلق طيرانهن إلا الله.

**Issue: Whether the ayah proves that voluntary acts of creatures are created by God**
- View 1 (al-Zuhayli, citing Ash'ari theologians): The ayah is evidence that الأفعال الاختيارية للعبد مخلوقة لله, because the bird's remaining airborne is an اختياري act, yet God attributes it to Himself.
- View 2 (Mu'tazili position, reported by al-Maturidi): the Mu'tazila denied that God creates acts of volition, reading إمساك as تعليم. Al-Maturidi devotes a long passage refuting this, arguing it undermines five Quranic purposes: proof of البعث, proof of الوحدانية, proof of الحكمة, proof of العلم, and proof of النعمة.

**Issue: The referent of مَن فِي السَّمَاءِ (in the preceding context, ayahs 16-17)**
- View 1 (al-Qurtubi, majority): God's سلطان and ملكوت are in the sky, not His physical being. The sky is mentioned because it is the seat of His command, throne, and angels.
- View 2 (al-Zamakhshari): the Quraysh believed in تشبيه (anthropomorphism), assuming God was physically in the sky. The Quran addressed them according to their own belief (على حسب اعتقادهم), while affirming that God is متعال عن المكان.
- View 3 (al-Zuhayli, citing al-Razi): the text cannot be taken literally because it would make God smaller than the sky, which is smaller than the عرش. The best تأويل: "the One whose سلطان and ملك are in the sky."

### Unique Insights

- **Ibn Ashur's "elephant analogy"**: He offers a striking epistemological point. A Tunisian or Maghrebi who first sees an elephant as an adult perceives its wonders more keenly than a person from India who grew up among elephants. Similarly, humans overlook the miracle of bird flight because they have seen it from infancy. The Quran's إطناب is designed to break through this habituation.
- **Ibn Ashur's link between إمساك and the fate of earlier nations**: The God who holds birds from هُوِيّ (plummeting to their death) is the same God who destroyed the أمم السابقة. Had those nations not committed شرك and had they clung to obedience, God would have saved them from destruction just as He saves the birds from falling.
- **Al-Maturidi's five-fold argument**: The ayah is part of a broader Quranic proof structure that serves five purposes simultaneously: establishing God's قدرة على البعث, His وحدانية, His حكمة, His علم, and His إنعام. Al-Maturidi argues that the Mu'tazili denial of خلق الأفعال undermines all five.

## Ayah 20: أَمَّنْ هَذَا الَّذِي هُوَ جُنْدٌ لَكُمْ يَنْصُرُكُمْ مِنْ دُونِ الرَّحْمَنِ إِنِ الْكَافِرُونَ إِلَّا فِي غُرُورٍ

### Core Meaning

This ayah issues a direct challenge to the mushrikin: who is this supposed جُند (army, supporter) of yours that could defend you against the Most Merciful? The question is rhetorical and إنكاري (denunciatory), and the expected answer is: no one (al-Baghawi, al-Qurtubi, Ibn Kathir). Ibn Kathir explains the context: God addresses the mushrikin who worshipped others besides Him, seeking from them نصر (victory) and رزق (sustenance), and tells them that what they hoped for will never materialize. Al-Zamakhshari offers two readings of the اسم الإشارة (هذا): either it points to an imagined army that the listener is challenged to identify ("point to him and say: this is the one"), or it points directly to the أوثان (idols) that the mushrikin actually worshipped, believing they would protect them. Ibn Ashur develops the first reading at length: the إشارة is to a مفهوم مفروض في الأذهان, a hypothetical concept made present to the mind. The استفهام is used for تعجيز (rendering someone incapable of response), thereby implying that the supposed جند does not exist. Al-Maturidi adds that the mushrikin themselves knew their idols could not help in moments of real crisis, because when hardship struck they turned to God alone, as in وإذا مسّ الإنسان ضرّ دعا ربه منيبًا إليه. The ayah reminds them in times of ease (الأمن) what they already know in times of fear (الخوف).

The closing statement, إِنِ الْكَافِرُونَ إِلَّا فِي غُرُورٍ, is an اعتراض (parenthetical) according to Ibn Ashur, generalizing the judgment to all كافرون: they are immersed in غرور (delusion), whether from الشيطان (al-Baghawi, al-Qurtubi) or from their own misplaced trust in the أصنام (Ibn Ashur). Al-Zuhayli notes that the expression مِنْ دُونِ الرَّحْمنِ signals that mankind's continued existence despite their كفر is itself an act of رحمة from الرحمن.

### Linguistic Analysis

- **أمّن**: أم المنقطعة + مَن الاستفهامية. The أم is for الإضراب الانتقالي (transitional shift), moving from the proof of divine power (birds) to the challenge about idols (Ibn Ashur). In the مصحف, it is written as one word with a single مِيم after the همزة, representing the إدغام of the two ميمs.
- **جُنْد**: collective noun (يوحّد), hence هذا rather than هؤلاء (al-Qurtubi). Ibn Ashur explains the use of الجملة الاسمية (هو جند لكم) to indicate دوام and ثبوت: a جند must be in a permanent state of readiness, not just present during battle.
- **غرور**: ظنّ النفس وقوع أمر نافع لها بمخائل تتوهمها, the soul's assumption that something beneficial will occur, based on illusory indications (Ibn Ashur). The ظرفية (في غرور) is مجازية, depicting them as enveloped by delusion on all sides (Ibn Ashur).

### Points of Divergence

**Issue: The referent of هذا**
- View 1 (al-Zamakhshari, Ibn Ashur possibility 1): an imagined, hypothetical جند that the interlocutor is challenged to identify. The إشارة is to a مفهوم مفروض في الأذهان.
- View 2 (al-Zamakhshari, Ibn Ashur possibility 2, al-Maturidi): it points to the أوثان and أصنام themselves, which the mushrikin treated as their protectors.

### Unique Insights

- **Al-Maturidi's triple reading**: He proposes three complementary dimensions of the ayah. (1) تخويف and تهويل (intimidation). (2) تنبيه and تسفيه أحلامهم (awakening and exposing the foolishness of their reasoning). (3) بشارة for the Prophet with future نصر, which was historically fulfilled at the فتح مكة when the mushrikin were defeated and had no جند to save them.

## Ayah 21: أَمَّنْ هَذَا الَّذِي يَرْزُقُكُمْ إِنْ أَمْسَكَ رِزْقَهُ بَلْ لَجُّوا فِي عُتُوٍّ وَنُفُورٍ

### Core Meaning

This ayah complements the preceding one. If ayah 20 challenged the mushrikin on the question of قوة (strength and protection), ayah 21 challenges them on رزق (sustenance). Who would provide for you if God withheld His provision? Ibn Kathir states plainly: لا أحد يعطي ويمنع ويخلق ويرزق وينصر إلا الله. Al-Maturidi connects the رزق to both السماء (rain) and الأرض (vegetation): the mushrikin depended on both, and if God withheld the مطر or ceased to make the earth yield النبات, no one could substitute. Ibn Ashur notes this ayah is ناظر (looking back) to وكلوا من رزقه (ayah 15) on the principle of اللف والنشر المعكوس (inverse correlation between two sequences).

The closing بَلْ لَجُّوا فِي عُتُوٍّ وَنُفُورٍ shifts from questioning to describing their stubbornness. Al-Zamakhshari: تمادوا في عناد وشراد عن الحق لثقله عليهم. Al-Maturidi defines العاتي as المارد الشديد السفه. Ibn Ashur provides the fullest analysis: the بل is for إضراب or إبطال, and the passage is an استئناف بياني answering the implicit question "did the proofs and warnings not affect them?" The answer: no, they لجّوا (persisted) in عتوّ (arrogant defiance) and نفور (recoiling from truth). The ظرفية (في عتوّ) is مجازية, implying عتوّ and نفور surrounded them so thoroughly that no escape route to الحق remained.

### Linguistic Analysis

- **لجّوا** (root: ل ج ج): Ibn Ashur: from لجّ في الخصومة (to persist stubbornly in a dispute), from باب سمع. Al-Baghawi: تمادوا في الضلال.
- **عتوّ** (root: ع ت و): تكبّر and طغيان (al-Zuhayli), عناد (al-Zamakhshari). Al-Maturidi: المارد الشديد السفه, one who is recklessly defiant.
- **نفور** (root: ن ف ر): تباعد عن الحق (al-Baghawi, al-Zuhayli). Ibn Ashur: الاشمئزاز من الشيء والهروب منه, disgust and flight from something.
- **جواب الشرط** in إن أمسك رزقه is محذوف (elided), understood from the preceding context: فمن يرزقكم (al-Zuhayli).

### Points of Divergence

**Issue: Whether بل لجّوا is about the mushrikin specifically or a general description**
- View 1 (Majority): it describes the specific mushrikin addressed, reporting their response to the proofs.
- View 2 (Ibn Ashur): it functions as an استئناف بياني, a broader narrative answer to any listener who might wonder whether the arguments worked.

### Unique Insights

- **Al-Maturidi's psychological insight**: The mushrikin knew in their hearts that only God could provide, because when they experienced drought and famine (القحط والجدوبة), they turned to God in supplication, not to their idols. The ayah reminds them in prosperity of what they already concede in adversity.
- **Ibn Ashur's لف ونشر معكوس**: Ayah 20 (about نصر) corresponds to فامشوا في مناكبها (ayah 15, about the earth being made ذلول and traversable), while ayah 21 (about رزق) corresponds to وكلوا من رزقه (ayah 15). The order is reversed (معكوس).

## Ayah 22: أَفَمَنْ يَمْشِي مُكِبًّا عَلَى وَجْهِهِ أَهْدَى أَمَّنْ يَمْشِي سَوِيًّا عَلَى صِرَاطٍ مُسْتَقِيمٍ

### Core Meaning

God strikes a مثل (parable) contrasting two walkers: one who stumbles forward, face-down, and another who walks upright on a straight path. All mufassirun agree this is a تمثيل (analogy) for the كافر and the مؤمن respectively (Ibn Kathir, al-Qurtubi, al-Baghawi, al-Zuhayli, Safwat). The كافر is like a man walking hunched over (منحنيًا), head lowered (منكّسًا رأسه), unable to see left or right, stumbling constantly (Ibn Kathir: تائه حائر ضالّ). The مؤمن is like one walking upright (منتصب القامة) on a clear, straight road (Ibn Kathir: طريق واضح بيّن). Al-Zamakhshari enriches the image: the مكبّ walks in rough, uneven terrain (مكان معتاد غير مستو) full of depressions and rises, so he trips repeatedly and falls on his face. The سويّ walks either straight and safe from stumbling, or in a consistently level direction without veering. Al-Zamakhshari adds a third possibility: the مكبّ is the أعمى who cannot find the road and therefore gropes about (يعتسف), while the سويّ is the صحيح البصر who sees his way clearly.

Ibn Ashur provides the most original analysis. He argues that no previous mufassir adequately explained why the first مثل necessarily corresponds to the mushrikin rather than the believers, since each side could claim to be the سويّ. His answer lies in the number of paths. The mushrik worships multiple scattered deities, unsure which one is stronger or more favorable to his tribe (comparing Thaqif's worship of اللات, the Aws and Khazraj's worship of مناة, etc.). He is like a traveler on branching, unmarked paths, bending down to examine faint footprints (آثار أقدام الناس) to guess which trail is traveled. The موحّد, by contrast, worships One God with certainty and walks confidently forward. This is illuminated by وأنّ هذا صراطي مستقيمًا فاتّبعوه ولا تتّبعوا السبل فتفرّق بكم عن سبيله (al-An'am 153).

Safwat notes this is an استعارة تمثيلية (extended allegorical metaphor). The مثل works both in الدنيا and الآخرة: in the world, the believer walks by guidance while the disbeliever wanders blindly; on the Day of Judgment, the believer will be resurrected walking upright on a مستقيم path to الجنة, while the disbeliever will be dragged on his face toward النار (Ibn Kathir, Qatada).

### Linguistic Analysis

- **مُكِبًّا** (root: ك ب ب): اسم فاعل from أكبّ (intransitive: "to be in a state of كبّ"). Al-Zamakhshari highlights a rare morphological pattern: كبّه (transitive, with no hamza) means "to topple him," but أكبّ (with hamza, intransitive) means "to enter a state of being toppled." This is from the same pattern as أقشع السحاب (the clouds cleared) from قشعت الريح (the wind cleared). Al-Zamakhshari insists this is one of the شواذ of Arabic morphology: the مطاوع of a فعل form does not normally take the أفعل pattern. He states that only those who have mastered كتاب سيبويه can properly understand this derivation. Ibn Ashur follows this analysis, citing analogies: أنفض القوم (their livestock perished), أرملوا (their provisions ran out).
- **سويًّا** (root: س و ي): فعيل بمعنى فاعل, i.e., مستوٍ (upright, balanced). Al-Zamakhshari: قائمًا سالمًا من العثور والخرور.
- **أهدى**: اسم تفضيل from الهدى. Ibn Ashur argues it is مسلوب المفاضلة (stripped of real comparison), because the مكبّ has no هداية at all, so the تفضيل is rhetorical. This is similar to السجن أحبّ إليّ (Yusuf 33), where prison is not genuinely "preferred," because what is offered is worse than prison. There is an element of تهكّم (sarcasm) or تمليح (ironic understatement).

**Rhetorical devices:**

- **استعارة تمثيلية**: Safwat and al-Zuhayli identify this as the primary بلاغة device. The entire scene is a composite metaphor.
- **إيجاز حذف**: Ibn Ashur notes that the صراط in the first half is omitted (the مكبّ walks on no stated path), and its character as معوجّ (crooked) is understood only from the contrast with مستقيم in the second half.
- **الفاء** in أفمن is للتفريع (consequential), building on everything from the beginning of the surah (Ibn Ashur).

### Points of Divergence

**Issue: Specific individuals or general categories?**
- View 1 (Ibn Abbas, al-Kalbi): the مكبّ is Abu Jahl specifically, and the سويّ is the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him).
- View 2 (Other reports): the سويّ is Hamza ibn Abd al-Muttalib (al-Zamakhshari), or Abu Bakr (al-Qurtubi), or Ammar ibn Yasir (Ikrimah, cited by al-Qurtubi).
- View 3 (Majority, including Ibn Kathir, al-Zuhayli, Safwat): it is عام (general), applying to every كافر and every مؤمن.

**Issue: Dunya or Akhira?**
- View 1 (Ibn Abbas, cited by al-Qurtubi): this is about الدنيا, a description of their present spiritual states.
- View 2 (Qatada, cited by al-Baghawi, Ibn Kathir): it is about الآخرة. The كافر أكبّ على المعاصي في الدنيا فحشره الله يوم القيامة على وجهه. The مؤمن walked straight in the world and will walk straight on the يوم القيامة.
- View 3 (Ibn Kathir, Safwat, al-Zuhayli): it applies to both الدنيا and الآخرة simultaneously, as a مثل of present spiritual reality and future eschatological reality.

**Issue: Physical or metaphorical walking?**
- View 1 (al-Zamakhshari): primarily metaphorical. The مكبّ represents one who يعتسف (blunders through rough terrain), a figure for spiritual waywardness.
- View 2 (Qatada): eschatological and literal. The Prophet (peace be upon him) was asked how people could be حشر on their faces, and he replied: أليس الذي أمشاهم على أرجلهم قادرًا على أن يمشيهم على وجوههم (Bukhari and Muslim, cited by Ibn Kathir).

### Unique Insights

- **Ibn Ashur's "multiple paths" explanation**: He is the only mufassir to articulate why the mushrik is necessarily the مكبّ: because polytheism means groping between multiple deities on multiple crooked paths, while توحيد is a single, straight, certain road. He supports this from أأرباب متفرّقون خير أم الله الواحد القهّار (Yusuf 39).
- **Al-Zamakhshari's morphological excursus on أكبّ**: His insistence that only readers of Sibawayh can properly understand the derivation is characteristic of his linguistic confidence. He identifies the rare pattern where the مجرّد (كبّ) is متعدٍّ but the مهموز (أكبّ) is لازم, the reverse of the normal pattern.
- **Al-Maturidi's theological extension**: He draws from this ayah a proof of البعث. If both the ضالّ and the مهتدي pass through this world with the same outward conditions, and if their claims about each other's error are mutually contradictory, then there must be a دار أخرى for الجزاء where the truth of each claim is settled.

## Ayah 23: قُلْ هُوَ الَّذِي أَنْشَأَكُمْ وَجَعَلَ لَكُمُ السَّمْعَ وَالْأَبْصَارَ وَالْأَفْئِدَةَ قَلِيلًا مَا تَشْكُرُونَ

### Core Meaning

God commands the Prophet (peace be upon him) to declare: He is the One who created you from nothing and gave you hearing, sight, and hearts, yet how little you give thanks. Ibn Kathir explains أنشأكم as ابتدأ خلقكم بعد أن لم تكونوا شيئًا مذكورًا (initiated your creation when you were nothing worth mentioning). The three faculties, السمع والأبصار والأفئدة, are singled out because they are أداة العلم والفهم (the instruments of knowledge and comprehension), as noted by Safwat and al-Zuhayli. Al-Maturidi expands: السمع allows knowledge of what is غائب (absent, distant), الأبصار allow knowledge of what is حاضر (present, visible), and الأفئدة enable apprehension of حقائق الأشياء ومبادئ الأمور ومآلها (the realities of things, the beginnings and outcomes of affairs). These three together are the comprehensive apparatus through which humans access all types of knowledge.

The closing قليلًا ما تشكرون is, according to Ibn Ashur, a كناية (metonymy) for complete absence of gratitude, not a statement that gratitude exists in small quantity. Al-Qurtubi and al-Baghawi interpret similarly: لا يشكرون هذه النعم ولا يوحّدون الله (they do not thank God for these blessings and do not affirm His oneness). The real شكر, as al-Zuhayli explains, is not verbal repetition but the صرف of each نعمة toward the purpose for which God created it: using السمع to hear the truth, البصر to see God's signs, and الفؤاد to reason toward God's oneness.

Al-Maturidi offers a remarkable theological meditation: God created the human being في أظلم مكان وأضيق موضع (in the darkest, narrowest place, i.e., the womb), where no human science or craft can reach, and there He fashioned the organs of perception. This proves five things simultaneously: (1) His قدرة surpasses all created power, (2) His علم encompasses what is hidden as fully as what is manifest, (3) He is متعالٍ عن الأشباه (transcendent above all analogies), since He acts simultaneously in countless wombs across creation without being present in any location, (4) He is المنعم (the Benefactor), who nourished and protected the embryo from all harm, and (5) His حكمة (wisdom) is infinite, since no sensible being creates something it knows will never serve its purpose.

### Linguistic Analysis

- **أنشأكم** (root: ن ش أ): الإيجاد, bringing into existence (Ibn Ashur). Ibn Kathir: ابتدأ خلقكم.
- **السمع**: مصدر, hence إفراد (singular), meaning the faculty of hearing. الأبصار: جمع بصر, meaning the eyes as organs (Ibn Ashur). الأفئدة: جمع فؤاد, meaning القلوب and by extension العقول (al-Zuhayli, Ibn Kathir, al-Qurtubi).
- **قليلًا**: نعت لمصدر محذوف (al-Zuhayli): تشكرون شكرًا قليلًا. **ما**: زائدة (al-Zuhayli, Ibn Ashur). Ibn Ashur classifies قليلًا as a صفة مشبّهة used in the sense of نفي (negation), a form of كناية and تمليح (understatement).
- **القصر** in هو الذي أنشأكم: قصر إفراد (Ibn Ashur), treating the mushrikin as if they believed the أصنام participated in إنشاء and granting of the senses.

**Rhetorical devices:**

- The shift from God speaking directly (in previous ayahs) to commanding the Prophet to speak (قل) is a تفنّن في البيان (rhetorical variation) and ترفيع لقدر النبيّ (elevation of the Prophet's status), making him a co-participant in the تذكير (Ibn Ashur).

### Points of Divergence

**Issue: Does قليلًا mean "a little" or "not at all"?**
- View 1 (Majority, including al-Qurtubi, Muqatil cited by al-Baghawi): it means لا يشكرون (they do not give thanks at all). The word قليلًا is used as a كناية for complete negation.
- View 2 (Literal reading, implicit in al-Zuhayli): they give thanks in a tiny, insufficient measure, using their faculties for worldly gain but not for worship.

### Unique Insights

- **Al-Maturidi's refutation of the Mu'tazila on شكر**: The Mu'tazila held that God is obligated to do what is أصلح (best) for His servants, so creating them with these faculties is merely fulfilling an obligation. If so, God is a قاضي حقّ (one discharging a debt), and a debtor who pays what he owes is not a benefactor who deserves شكر. Al-Maturidi rejects this: the Quranic demand for شكر (قليلًا ما تشكرون) proves that creation is an act of فضل (grace), not واجب (obligation).
- **Al-Maturidi on God's transcendence from مكان**: That God simultaneously creates in countless wombs across the earth proves He is not in any particular مكان. If He had to be present at the site of His action, He could not act in multiple places at once. This is proven by the verse إنما قولنا لشيء إذا أردناه أن نقول له كن فيكون.

## Ayah 24: قُلْ هُوَ الَّذِي ذَرَأَكُمْ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَإِلَيْهِ تُحْشَرُونَ

### Core Meaning

This ayah moves from the creation of the individual to the distribution and multiplication of humanity across the earth, and then to the ultimate gathering (الحشر). Ibn Kathir explains ذرأكم as بثّكم ونشركم في أقطار الأرض وأرجائها (He scattered you and spread you across the regions and extremities of the earth), adding: مع اختلاف ألسنتكم في لغاتكم وألوانكم (with the diversity of your languages and colors). Ibn Ashur refines the meaning: الذرء is الإكثار من الموجود (multiplying from existing stock), making it more specific than إنشاء (which is creation from nothing). Thus ayah 23 speaks of the original creation (أنشأكم), and ayah 24 speaks of the subsequent proliferation and dispersion (ذرأكم). Al-Qurtubi cites Ibn Abbas: ذرأكم means خلقكم, while Ibn Shajara says it means نشركم فيها وفرّقكم على ظهرها (He dispersed and distributed you on its surface).

The closing وإليه تحشرون carries two layers. On the surface, it announces the البعث and الحشر. Ibn Ashur finds a subtle كناية (metonymy with two degrees of indirection): الحشر implies البعث, and البعث implies الموت, so the mention of الحشر is an indirect reminder of الموت, which they already know is inevitable. This mirrors the surah's opening theme of الموت والحياة. Al-Maturidi notes the rhetorical strategy of joining a contested claim (الحشر, which some كفّار denied) with an uncontested one (ذرأكم, which all acknowledge), so that the proof of the uncontested serves as evidence for the contested: the One who scattered you can reassemble you.

### Linguistic Analysis

- **ذرأكم** (root: ذ ر أ): al-Zuhayli: خلقكم متكاثرين موزّعين. Ibn Ashur: الذرء is الإكثار من الموجود, making it distinct from الإنشاء and الخلق.
- **تُحْشَرُونَ**: مبني للمجهول (passive). Al-Zuhayli: تجمعون للحساب والجزاء.
- **تقديم المعمول** in وإليه تحشرون: Ibn Ashur explains this is للاهتمام and لرعاية الفاصلة (attention and care for the verse-ending pattern), not للاختصاص, because the mushrikin did not claim حشر to any deity at all; they denied the concept entirely.
- **القصر** in هو الذي ذرأكم: same structure as ayah 23, قصر إفراد (Ibn Ashur).

### Points of Divergence

**Issue: The relationship between أنشأكم (ayah 23) and ذرأكم (ayah 24)**
- View 1 (Ibn Ashur): they are distinct. أنشأ = original creation from nothing. ذرأ = multiplication and dispersion of existing creation.
- View 2 (al-Qurtubi, citing Ibn Abbas): ذرأكم simply means خلقكم (created you), with في الأرض indicating the location, making it largely synonymous with أنشأكم but with a spatial dimension.
- View 3 (al-Zuhayli): ذرأكم = خلقكم متكاثرين موزّعين (created you in a state of multiplying and being distributed), combining creation and dispersion.

### Unique Insights

- **Ibn Kathir's emphasis on diversity**: He is the only mufassir in this group who explicitly mentions اختلاف الألسنة and الألوان and الحلى and الأشكال and الصور (diversity of languages, colors, features, forms, and appearances) as part of the ذرء, echoing the theme of Surat al-Rum 30:22.
- **Ibn Ashur's double كناية**: The mention of الحشر is a كناية for الموت by two steps of logical implication. This is a sophisticated بلاغة observation: the reader moves from the explicit (gathering) to the implicit first step (resurrection) to the implicit second step (death), and feels the full weight of mortality.
- **Al-Maturidi's argument from the uncontested to the contested**: By placing the universally acknowledged fact of human dispersion (ذرأكم في الأرض) alongside the disputed claim of الحشر, the Quran makes the proof of God's creative power serve as evidence for His power to reassemble, following the Quranic principle: وهو الذي يبدأ الخلق ثم يعيده وهو أهون عليه.
- **Repetition of قُلْ**: Ibn Ashur notes the repetition of قل without a عاطف (conjunction) is a form of تكرير for اهتمام (emphasis), signaling that each statement deserves its own full proclamation.
